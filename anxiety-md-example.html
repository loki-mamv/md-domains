Understanding Anxiety

What is anxiety?

Anxiety is your body's natural response to stress ‚Äî a feeling of fear or apprehension about what's to come. While everyone experiences anxiety, anxiety disorders involve more than temporary worry or fear.

For people with anxiety disorders, the anxiety does not go away and can get worse over time. The symptoms can interfere with daily activities such as job performance, schoolwork, and relationships.

Types

Types of anxiety disorders

Anxiety disorders differ by the objects or situations that induce them, but share features of excessive anxiety and related behavioral disturbances.

Treatment

Evidence-based treatments

Anxiety disorders are highly treatable. Several effective, research-backed treatment options exist:

  • 01

    Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT)

    The gold standard for anxiety treatment. Helps identify and change negative thought patterns. Most people see significant improvement in 12-16 sessions.

  • 02

    Medication

    SSRIs, SNRIs, and in some cases benzodiazepines can help manage symptoms. Always prescribed and monitored by a psychiatrist or physician.

  • 03

    Exposure Therapy

    Gradually and systematically confronting feared situations. Particularly effective for phobias and social anxiety disorder.

  • 04

    Lifestyle Modifications

    Regular exercise, adequate sleep, limiting caffeine, and stress management techniques like mindfulness meditation.
