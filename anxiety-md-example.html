What Is Anxiety?

Anxiety is a normal emotion characterized by feelings of tension, worried thoughts, and physical changes like increased blood pressure. While everyone experiences anxiety, anxiety disorders involve more than temporary worry or fear.

For people with anxiety disorders, the anxiety does not go away and can get worse over time. The symptoms can interfere with daily activities such as job performance, schoolwork, and relationships.

Treatment Options

Anxiety disorders are highly treatable, yet only about 37% of those suffering receive treatment. Several effective treatment options exist:

  • Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT) ‚Äî The gold standard for anxiety treatment. Helps identify and change negative thought patterns.
  • Medication ‚Äî SSRIs, SNRIs, and benzodiazepines can help manage symptoms. Always consult a psychiatrist.
  • Lifestyle Changes ‚Äî Regular exercise, adequate sleep, limiting caffeine, and stress management techniques.
  • Mindfulness & Relaxation ‚Äî Meditation, deep breathing, and progressive muscle relaxation can reduce symptoms.
